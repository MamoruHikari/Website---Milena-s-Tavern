Classic Apple Pie with a Scoop of Vanilla Ice Cream

Apple pie is a timeless dessert that brings warmth and comfort with every bite. Paired with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, this classic treat becomes an irresistible delight. Perfect for gatherings or a cozy night in, this recipe will have everyone asking for seconds.

The Charm of Apple Pie

Apple pie isn’t just a dessert; it’s a symbol of home and tradition. The flaky, buttery crust combined with a sweet and tart apple filling creates a perfect balance of textures and flavors. Topped with creamy vanilla ice cream, each bite offers a delightful contrast of warm and cold, crispy and smooth.

Why Apples are Perfect for Pies

Apples are the star of this dessert, providing a natural sweetness and a firm texture that holds up well during baking. They are also packed with dietary fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants, making this indulgent treat a bit more wholesome.

Step-by-Step Guide to Classic Apple Pie with Vanilla Ice Cream

  1. Prepare the Crust: Combine flour, salt, and sugar in a bowl. Cut in cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add ice water, mixing until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape into discs, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least an hour.
  2. Make the Filling: Peel, core, and slice the apples. Toss them with sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  3. Roll Out the Dough: On a floured surface, roll out one disc of dough to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Transfer the dough to the plate and trim the edges.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Fill the crust with the apple mixture. Dot with butter. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim, seal, and flute the edges. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Cover the edges with foil if they brown too quickly.
  6. Serve: Let the pie cool slightly before serving. Slice and top each piece with a generous scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Nutritional Highlights

This classic apple pie offers a combination of carbohydrates, fiber, and healthy fats, making it a delicious treat that satisfies your sweet tooth and provides some nutritional benefits.

  • Apples: Rich in dietary fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants.
  • Butter: Adds richness and helps create a flaky crust.
  • Cinnamon: Contains antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties.

Health Benefits of Apples

Including apples in your diet can support heart health, aid in digestion, and help regulate blood sugar levels. The dietary fiber in apples promotes a healthy gut and can keep you feeling full longer.

Research Insights

Studies have shown that regularly consuming apples can improve heart health. Research in the British Journal of Nutrition found that apple consumption is associated with a lower risk of cardiovascular disease.

Conclusion

Classic apple pie with a scoop of vanilla ice cream is a dessert that combines tradition and indulgence. With its perfectly flaky crust and sweet, spiced apple filling, this pie is sure to become a favorite. The addition of creamy vanilla ice cream enhances the flavors and provides a satisfying finish to this delightful treat.

Did you know?

The warm apple pie and cold vanilla ice cream create a delightful temperature contrast that enhances the flavors and textures, making each bite a truly memorable experience.
